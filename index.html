Safety Equipment & Protection

Safety equipment helps protect workers from hazards, accidents, injuries, and emergencies in the workplace.

1. Fire Extinguisher

Used to control or extinguish small fires during emergencies. Always know the nearest fire extinguisher location.

Safety Route

5S Workplace Organization Methodology

The 5S Methodology is a workplace organization system designed to improve efficiency, safety, cleanliness, and productivity.

1. Sort (Seiri)

Remove unnecessary items from the workspace so only essential tools and materials remain.

2. Set in Order (Seiton)

Arrange necessary items properly for easy access and efficient workflow.

3. Shine (Seisō)

Clean the workspace regularly to maintain safety and detect hazards.

4. Standardize (Seiketsu)

Create consistent procedures and schedules to maintain organization.

5. Sustain (Shitsuke)

Build discipline and continuous improvement habits among employees.

Dengue Prevention & Awareness

Dengue is a mosquito-borne disease that can be prevented through proper sanitation, cleanliness, and awareness.

1. Eliminate Standing Water

Remove stagnant water from containers, tires, flower pots, and canals where mosquitoes can breed.

2. Maintain Clean Surroundings

Keep workplaces and communities clean to reduce mosquito breeding areas.

3. Use Protection

Wear long sleeves and use mosquito repellents or nets to avoid mosquito bites.

4. Watch for Symptoms

Common symptoms include fever, headache, body pain, nausea, and skin rashes.

5. Seek Medical Attention

Consult a doctor immediately if dengue symptoms appear to avoid severe complications.

Earthquake Safety & Preparedness

Earthquakes can happen unexpectedly. Proper preparation and quick action can help reduce injuries and save lives.

1. Prepare an Emergency Kit

Keep essential supplies such as water, food, flashlight, batteries, medicines, and first aid kits ready.

2. Drop, Cover, and Hold

During an earthquake, get under a sturdy table, protect your head, and hold on until the shaking stops.

3. Follow Evacuation Routes

Move calmly to designated evacuation areas after the shaking stops and avoid crowded exits.

4. Stay Away from Hazards

Avoid damaged buildings, glass windows, electrical wires, and unstable structures after an earthquake.

5. Check for Injuries and Damage

Help injured individuals if safe to do so and report dangerous conditions immediately.

Electrical Safety Awareness

Electrical hazards can cause serious injuries, fires, and equipment damage. Following proper safety procedures helps maintain a safe workplace.

1. Inspect Equipment Regularly

Check electrical cords, plugs, and tools for damage before use to prevent accidents and short circuits.

2. Avoid Wet Conditions

Never handle electrical devices with wet hands or near water to reduce the risk of electric shock.

3. Prevent Overloading

Avoid plugging too many devices into one outlet or extension cord to prevent overheating and fires.

4. Use Proper Protective Equipment

Wear insulated gloves and safety gear when working near electrical systems or machinery.

5. Know Emergency Procedures

Learn how to shut off power sources and respond quickly during electrical emergencies.

Tsunami Safety & Preparedness

A tsunami is a series of powerful waves usually caused by underwater earthquakes. These waves can travel very fast and become dangerous when reaching coastal areas.

1. Know the Warning Signs

Signs include strong earthquakes, sudden sea level changes, and roaring sounds coming from the ocean.

2. Move to Higher Ground

Immediately leave low-lying coastal areas after a strong earthquake and proceed to safer elevated places.

3. Stay Away from the Shoreline

Never go near the beach to watch the tsunami because the waves can travel faster than expected.

4. Watch for Unusual Sea Conditions

Rapid lowering of sea water may indicate an incoming tsunami. Move to higher areas immediately.

5. Stay Prepared and Informed

Since the Philippines is located along the Pacific Ring of Fire, awareness and quick action are important for safety.

Final Reminder

Earthquakes and tsunamis are common natural hazards in the Philippines. Through awareness, preparedness, and fast action, we can protect ourselves and others.

Stay aware. Stay safe. Stay prepared.
